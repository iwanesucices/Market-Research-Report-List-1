<p><p>The Vitamin D therapy market is categorized into two primary types: oral route drugs and parenteral route drugs. Oral route drugs include supplements such as capsules, tablets, and liquids that are ingested, making them convenient for daily use. Parenteral route drugs involve injections, which are typically administered for rapid absorption and are often used in cases where oral intake is insufficient or impractical. Both types aim to address deficiencies and support overall health, particularly bone and immune function.</p></p>
