<p><p>The audio power amplifier IC market is segmented into various classes based on operational characteristics. Class A amplifiers offer high fidelity but are less efficient. Class B amplifiers improve efficiency but can introduce distortion at low volumes. Class C amplifiers are highly efficient and primarily used for RF applications. Class AB amplifiers combine the benefits of A and B, balancing performance and efficiency. Class D amplifiers utilize digital techniques for high efficiency and are popular in consumer electronics, particularly for portable devices.</p></p>
