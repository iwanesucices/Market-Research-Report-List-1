<p><p>Transformers in switching power supplies are classified into single-excited and double-excited types based on their operation. Single-excited transformers use a single primary winding to generate magnetic flux, suitable for low-power applications. In contrast, double-excited transformers feature two windings that allow for higher efficiency and performance, making them ideal for higher power demands. These classifications cater to different market needs, balancing factors such as efficiency, size, and application requirements in the power supply sector.</p></p>
