<p><p>PTA Balloon Dilatation Catheters are medical devices used in interventional procedures to treat vascular conditions, primarily in the management of peripheral artery disease (PAD) and coronary artery disease (CAD). These catheters consist of a balloon that is inflated at the site of a blockage to widen narrowed blood vessels, thereby improving blood flow and reducing symptoms associated with these conditions. <p><p>PTA balloon dilatation catheters are classified based on their rated burst pressure, which indicates the pressure at which the balloon will rupture. The market includes categories such as: </p><p>1. Below 10 Atm: For lower-pressure applications, often used in less severe lesions. </p><p>2. 10-20 Atm: Suitable for moderate lesions, providing a balance of strength and flexibility. </p><p>3. 20-30 Atm: Designed for more challenging cases, ensuring effective dilation in critical stenosis. </p><p>4. Above 30 Atm: High-pressure balloons for severe lesions, offering maximum dilation capability and durability. These classifications help clinicians choose appropriate devices based on the severity of the vascular condition.</p></p>
