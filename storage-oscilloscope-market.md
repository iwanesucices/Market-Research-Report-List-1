<p><p>The storage oscilloscope market is primarily divided into digital and analog oscilloscopes. Digital oscilloscopes capture and store waveforms as digital signals, enabling advanced features like data analysis, storage, and retrieval for complex signal evaluation. They offer greater accuracy and versatility in measurements. In contrast, analog oscilloscopes display waveforms in real time on a screen, providing immediate visual feedback, but with limited processing capabilities. While digital oscilloscopes dominate due to their functionality, analog models are still valued for their simplicity and real-time performance.</p></p>
